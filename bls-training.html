Emergency Response

Choking: First 60 Seconds

Unable to cry, speak, or cough? Act now.

⏱ 60s Window
▶️

Watch: How to perform the Heimlich maneuver

Infants (Under 1 Year)

1

Hold baby face-down along your forearm, head lower than body.

2

Give 5 firm back blows between shoulder blades.

3

Flip and give 5 chest thrusts using two fingers.

Children (Over 1 Year)

1

Stand behind the child and wrap arms around waist.

2

Place fist just above the belly button.

3

Give quick inward & upward thrusts until object is out.

❌ DO NOT DO THIS

Do not put fingers blindly into the mouth (you might push it deeper). Do not give water. Do not wait to "see if it passes."

Home Care

Nosebleed: First 10 Minutes

Stay calm—it looks like more blood than it actually is.

1

Sit upright: Lean slightly FORWARD. Never tilt the head back.

2

The Pinch: Firmly pinch the soft part of the nose for 10 minutes straight.

3

No Checking: Don't let go to "check" if it stopped—this breaks the clot.

✅ CALL 1066 IF:

Bleeding continues after 20 mins, or if it follows a heavy fall/head injury.

Expert: Dr. Namrata (BSK)

Seizures: Protecting the Airway

1. Side-Lying: Gently turn the child onto their side to keep the airway clear.

2. Clear Space: Move furniture or hard objects away.

3. Time It: If the seizure lasts >5 minutes, it is a medical emergency.

❌ NEVER

Do not restrain the child or put anything (spoons, fingers) in their mouth.
